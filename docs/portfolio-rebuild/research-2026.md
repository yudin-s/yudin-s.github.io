# Portfolio Research 2026

## Practical Patterns

1. Hero should behave like a product interface, not only a greeting.
2. Expert portfolios are increasingly case-led: results, role, constraints, stack, and proof matter more than a long skills list.
3. Motion should guide scanning: entrance transitions, hover previews, scroll reveals, and one signature interactive moment.
4. Strong composition comes from grid rhythm, modular density, and typography rather than decorative effects.
5. Senior positioning should communicate judgment: architecture, reliability, performance, delivery, and open-source proof.
6. WebGL/3D is useful only when it adds memorability without hurting readability, accessibility, or performance.
7. Reduced motion, keyboard focus, mobile fit, stable layout, and Lighthouse-friendly performance are part of the 2026 standard.

## Directional Takeaway

For this portfolio, the best fit is not an agency-style spectacle. It should feel like a senior engineer's product system: confident, fast, precise, lightly animated, with one memorable interactive layer and strong proof blocks.
